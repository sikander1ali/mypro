About Us

Gilgit-Baltistan, a region situated in the northern part of Pakistan, is renowned for its rich cultural heritage and the production of high-quality dry fruits. The area's unique geography and climate contribute to the cultivation of a variety of dry fruits that have been a significant part of the local economy and trade for centuries. One of the most prized dry fruits from Gilgit-Baltistan is apricot, known locally as "Chulli." The apricots grown in the region are celebrated for their exceptional taste and nutritional value. The locals have mastered the art of sun-drying these apricots, preserving their natural sweetness and creating a delicacy that is not only consumed locally but also exported to various parts of the world. Another notable dry fruit from the region is the walnut. Gilgit-Baltistan is home to vast walnut orchards, and the nuts produced here are renowned for their size and flavor. Walnuts play a vital role in the local cuisine, finding their way into traditional dishes and desserts. The cultivation and trade of dry fruits have historical roots in Gilgit-Baltistan, dating back to the ancient Silk Road era. The region's strategic location along these ancient trade routes facilitated the exchange of goods, including precious dry fruits, with neighboring regions and distant lands. Today, the dry fruit industry in Gilgit-Baltistan continues to thrive, contributing significantly to the economic prosperity of the region. The cultivation and processing of apricots, walnuts, and other dry fruits have become a source of livelihood for many local communities. Moreover, the unique flavor and quality of Gilgit-Baltistan's dry fruits have gained recognition and popularity in national and international markets. As the region continues to preserve its cultural heritage and promote sustainable agricultural practices, the story of Gilgit-Baltistan's dry fruits stands as a testament to the rich traditions and economic importance of this mountainous terrain.
